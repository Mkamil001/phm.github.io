Mrs. Crystal Ameyaw – (Founder and Director)

About Us

HAVING OVER 10 YEARS EXPERIENCE

History often reveals that legacies are born of humble beginnings and human desires to make the world a better place to live and nurture our families. In 2012, Mrs. Crystal Ameyaw founded Precious Hearts Montessori (PHM) in response to a local cry around the Adenta-Oyibi-Dodowa-Ayikumah stretch for an alternative to traditional public education.

Defining moments are rare. Rarer still are the organizations and individuals that rise to the challenge and establish a legacy that has the potential to stand the test of time. From its humble beginnings as a one-room school in a house to its current 200-plus enrollment, PHM has undergone many changes in its eventful lifetime! PHM has undergone significant change and transformation over the course of its 10-year history. PHM has evolved from humble beginnings into a substantive, independent School that is a great place for children to learn and grow. Sensorial and motor development are the child’s means of exploration in the early years – here the Montessori theory agrees with Jean Piaget, and so Mrs. Ameyaw advocated giving the children room to explore. She believed that a child’s independence would grow from choosing his/her activities wisely and with the help of an adult guide. Furthermore, cooperation with others and responsibility for group tasks is emphasized, as it instills important values that are derived from working with others. Children become self-regulated through concentration on stimulating self-chosen tasks that they can pursue individually or in groups. Montessori called this process “normalization.” She also extolled the idea that the Montessori approach encourages self-discipline, self-knowledge, independence, academic skills, problem-solving ability, and an enthusiasm for learning which forms the basis for the vision and mission of PHM. Demand from within the PHM community, other well-meaning people, and schools offering a primary Montessori stream for an extended pathway to primary school lead to the establishment of the primary and Junior High School (JHS) section, all with the Montessori ideology
